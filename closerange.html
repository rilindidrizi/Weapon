Explanation

  • 9X19 mm. It means that the bullet used for the gun is 9 mm in diameter and 19 mm long.
  • Short recoil. In short recoil bolt and barrel remain together only a small distance, usually a few millimeters for pistols or the distance of a few calibers for machine guns.
  • Recoil operated. Recoil operation is an operating mechanism used to implement locked-breech, autoloading firearms. Recoil operated firearms use the energy of recoil to cycle the action.
  • Rotating bolt. This means that the weapon loads itself after one bullet is fired. When you fire the first bullet, cartridge gets out of the bullet without touching it. After this is done the other bullet locks to the place it should be and it is ready to get fired.
  • Muzzle velocity. Is the speed of the bullet after it is fired.
  • Feed system. It tells the number of the bullets a gun can hold. Usuallu guns have different variations of what is known as the feed system.
  • .45 ACP .40S&W 10mm Auto. They are types of bullets. .45 ACP is a bullet with diameter of 11.43mm and length of 23mm. It was first used in Colt semi-automatic pistol. .40S&W is a bullet with diameter of 10mm and length of 22mm. It is rimless (rim is the lowest part of a bullet). 10mm Auto is a bullet with diameter of 10mm and length of 25mm.
  • Round magazine. It is a special type of a magazine. It holds more bullets than a normal magazine and it is longer than a normal magazine.
  • Drum magazine. It is also a special type of a magazine. It holds more bullets than both normal and round magazine. It is round and it is usually used in fully-automatic pistols.
  • .357 SIG. It is a bullet diameter of which is 9mm and the length of it is 22mm.
  • .22 Long Rifle (LR). It is a bullet with diameter of 5.6mm and length of 15mm.
  • 12-gauge. It is ammo used only for shotguns. The number before "gauge" tell you that this shotgun can shoot that much bullets of the same diameter from its barrel. The lower the number the stronger the shotgun.
  • .410 bore. It is the second smallest shotgun ammo.
  • 437-grain rifled slug. A slug is a projectile. Usually the shotgun bullets spread a lot but a projectile fires like a single bullet.
  • Gas-operated. Gas-operation is a system of operation used to provide energy to operate locked breech.
